What is Find Naija?
Find Naija is a search service app for discovering restaurants, hotels and attractions in Nigeria, West Africa.

Find Naija is also a crowd sourced review platform that enables its users to view and read reviews from people who have experienced the businesses first hand. The platform has a social feature, enabling users to follow people, like posts, comment, share pictures and more.

Overview

Problem

‍There is currently no search service app like Yelp in Nigeria, locating new businesses and service can be difficult for a variety of reasons. After speaking with a number of people who visit Nigeria frequently , they expressed the need for a search service app to help the process of discovering new places.

Solution
‍
‍Find Naija is a search service and crowd-sourced review platform optimized for mobile. It focuses mainly on these 3 categories restaurants, hotels and attractions. It will enable the user to discover new businesses to experience either by searching directly or using the corresponding icon to find businesses around their current location.

Competitive Analysis

Choosing 3 Search Service platforms in Nigeria using a SWOT analysis I was able to compare the features of Tripadvisor, Vconnect, and Hotel.ng.

TripAdvisor  was the obvious choice as the top competitor. It’s branding and presence among users is widely known and people use the service to search for everything. It’s positioned itself at the top and is backed by its number of reviews and the number of businesses on its application. It does face issues of review manipulation and poses a threat to smaller businesses. Vconnect focuses mostly on personal and professional services and Hotel.ng focuses exclusively on hotels. These two services are direct threats to a potential application but the lack of market presence still leaves room for opportunity for Find Naija to enter the market.

Usability Testing

Putting together my prototype in Invision, I created a usability test using the Steve Krug usability script as my template, I tested participants in these three areas 1. Creating an account on the app 2. Viewing their profile on the app 3. Searching for a hotel/restaurants using the corresponding icon. I received significant feedback during my usability test which included:
‍
1. Users wanted to preview the app before they had to sign up.
2. Users complained about how long it took them to scroll the dashboard/homepage.

Lesson Learned

Digital revolution remains one of the key highlights of the 21st century. Technology has helped in shaping the way things are done. It has brought innovations and inventions into service delivery. Nigeria’s technology revolution is growing at a progressive pace but still lags behind the global technology market. Find Naija is a tool that will enhance the way people in Nigeria discover new businesses, it’s a simple and intuitive interface that gives the user to ability to search for a location or click an icon to see recommended businesses in the area.

Throughout this process I learned so much about how digital products can improve the quality of life for people on a daily basis. The need for meaningful products like this, take Yelp for instance, as of September 30, 2018, 81% of searches done on yelp were done on a mobile device and 69% of reviews on yelp were generated on a mobile device. A majority of internet users in Nigeria access the internet on a mobile device, so Find Naija is strategically placed to capture a large market share in the region.

The app isn't perfect though, if given more time, there is room for the app to grow beyond the minimum viable product by including other services and businesses that people search for on a daily basis. The Usability test conducted shed light on something I overlooked, people want to take a look at your product, play around with it before committing to buy it or in this case create an account, since this is an iOS, I went back to read Apple’s human interface guidelines, the section that states “delay sign-in as long as possible... give them time to fall in love with your app before making a commitment to it”
