Overview

Problems
‍
• Organizing files can be complicated with a lot of files.
• Creating content on the storage app is not always possible.
• Saving links and images from the web requires a lot of steps.
• Real time collaboration is not efficient.
• Items sent by others often gets lost in the shuffle.

Solutions
‍
‍• Create categories to inspire Organization of files.
• Make creating content on the app easy and efficient.
• Ability to save links and images from the web on the app.
• Make assigning tasks between collaborators easy and intuitive.
• Option to accept or reject an invite or file on the app.
Process

Assumptions

The cloud storage market is still young and growing, there are different users of cloud storage tools; we have users who like to save everything on the cloud, users who only save important items and users who like store everything but in a highly organized manner.

Real cloud was designed to meet user’s at their need as they evolve with the right combination of features based on research and user feedback.

User Research

85% of participants are familiar with cloud storage apps like Google Drive, Dropbox.
82% of particpants use cloud storage daily as their primary means of storing files.
54% of participants use cloud storage for personal and business.
‍
89% of participants stated sharing content is important to them.
85% of participants stated organizing content is important to them.
73% of participants stated the ability to create content on the app is important.

Competitive Analysis

To get an idea of where Real Cloud will stand in the market of cloud storage, I did a thorough analysis of cloud storage and organization apps by completing a SWOT analysis.

The participants in my survey were most familiar with Google drive , Dropbox and Pinterest. Some of their strengths are sharing, collaborating, availability on all platforms.

Google drive has a wide array of integrated apps like google docs , Dropbox is very good at syncing files automatically and handling sharing permissions, Pinterest is strong in its visual representation of items and organization. It's simple and easy to use. Dropbox and Google drive are at the top of the cloud storage world and present an enormous hurdle to overcome. One feature that sits at the top of the client's needs is organization, this is where Pinterest shines, with its strong visual representation of items and organization. An integration of the features of all three of these applications makes for an interesting service.

Usability Testing

I conducted three usability test for my high fidelity prototypes. Two of the tests were done remotely and one of the test was done in person.   

The feedback for all the participants in the survey was that they were confused on how to create a document on the app, this was alarming since one of the main features of the app is the ability to create content like documents and spreadsheets. I took this in account and made some final changes to the design

Image 1
Image 2

Before testing my third user, I made a very subtle change to the information presented in the design. I changed the word Add (Image 1) to Create (Image 2). I was curious to see if the third user would encounter the same problem as the previous two testers. Watch the muted video below to see third user in action. Did the user encounter the same problem when trying to create a document?

You are correct. The third user was able to create a document on the app with relative ease. It's very interesting that a subtle change in how information is presented can change user experience

Lesson Learned

The cloud storage and organization industry is still young and growing, user needs are constantly evolving and changing.
‍
This was a very interesting project for me, the user surveys and research were are at the forefront of this design, at times I wanted to veer off and design what I thought looked good, I was quickly confronted by the research I had conducted in the process stage of this project.
‍
The user testing stage of the project was  instrumental in a critical category of the design of the app.  Based on feedback changing the word Add to Create, solved the issue users faced when trying to create a document or spreadsheet on the app.
‍
The app isn’t perfect though, if given more time I would team up with other designers and developers, there is much potential for this product to fulfill beyond the minimum viable product.
